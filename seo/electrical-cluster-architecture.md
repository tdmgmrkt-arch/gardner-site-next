### 1.5 Risk Notes (flags for writer/developer — no structure changes)

**Smart Home Solutions** — SERP pulls a mixed audience: electricians, smart home integrators (Crestron/Control4 installers), and big-box retailers. Competitor domain overlap with cluster peers is 2 (weakest in its cluster). Mitigation: Frame tightly as "electrical infrastructure for smart home devices" — dedicated circuits, smart panel compatibility, wiring for hubs and EV/solar-ready panels. Avoid "smart home automation" framing that competes with non-electrical integrators. Do NOT target "home automation installation" as a primary keyword.

**Generator Installation** — Scores 2–3 against cluster peers. Generator SERP pulls propane/gas generator dealers and HVAC contractors (for whole-home backup), not just electricians. This creates a natural cross-vertical bridge to HVAC (see §4). Mitigation: Frame as "standby generator wiring and connection" — the electrical hookup, transfer switch, and load management work, not the generator selection/purchase. This differentiates from dealer-side competition and aligns with what a plumbing-turned-multi-trade contractor credibly does.

**Pool & Spa Wiring** — Scores 3 against cluster peers. SERP mixes pool contractors with electricians. Mitigation: Frame as "electrical code compliance for pools and spas" (NEC Article 680 angle), bonding/grounding, GFCI requirements. This positions the page around electrical expertise rather than pool construction.

**Remodeling & New Construction** — Intent is broadly commercial; SERP is dominated by full electrical contractors rather than multi-trade companies. Mitigation: Frame around what a multi-trade company uniquely offers — coordinated electrical, plumbing, and HVAC rough-in for remodels. This is a genuine differentiator for Gardner and avoids direct competition with pure-play electricians on their strongest keyword.
