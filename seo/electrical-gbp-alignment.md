## 2. Three-Sub-Hub Grouping Sanity Check

The proposed structure — **Installation and Fixtures / Power Systems and Upgrades / Maintenance, Safety and Repairs** — is reasonable as a website IA but does not map cleanly to how Google categorizes electrical work. GBP does not subdivide "Electrician" the way Gardner's three-hub structure does. That is fine — the sub-hubs are for on-site user experience and content depth, not GBP taxonomy. One concern and one re-grouping flag:

**The coherence problem: "Electrician" is a flat category.** Unlike HVAC (where GBP has distinct categories for AC, heating, and air quality that map 1-to-1 to the three hubs), electrical has no equivalent sub-specialty categories in GBP with material search volume. All 14 electrical leaf services will attach to the single "Electrician" category in the GBP Services panel. This is fine — it just means the sub-hub structure is a website-side organizational choice, not a GBP signal.

**Rearrangement recommendation — one page only:** "Emergency Electrical Services" currently sits in the Maintenance, Safety and Repairs sub-hub. That is the right call for UX. For GBP Services, however, "Emergency Electrical Service" or "24-Hour Electrician" is a standalone high-intent service that should have its own Services entry rather than being grouped under a maintenance description. The page placement stays where it is; the GBP service entry needs to stand alone.

**"Remodeling and New Construction" belongs in Power Systems.** That is where it sits in the proposed structure. Confirm it stays there — it would be misplaced in Maintenance/Repairs and wrong in Installation/Fixtures. The Power Systems sub-hub is the right logical home even if the page name needs a flag (see Section 3).

## 4. Brand-Vertical Dissonance — Gardner Plumbing Doing Electrical

**This is a harder stretch than HVAC, and it compounds with HVAC.** Be direct about the risk:

With HVAC, the brand-name hesitation is real but has precedent — plumbers and HVAC techs are often the same company in residential service, and many homeowners know this. With electrical, that overlap expectation largely disappears. A searcher looking for "electrician Murrieta" who sees "Gardner Plumbing Co." in the map pack will hesitate harder than the HVAC searcher. The brand name signals a trade specialty, and electricity is not that trade.

**The compounding problem:** Gardner is now adding HVAC and electrical simultaneously. The GBP profile will show a business named "Gardner Plumbing" claiming to do plumbing, air conditioning, heating, air duct cleaning, and electrical work. For local searchers, that is either a full home services company (reassuring) or a plumber who has overextended (alarming). Which framing wins depends almost entirely on the review content and photo library.

**The DBA question is now more urgent.** With only HVAC added, "Gardner Plumbing and Air" was a reasonable DBA option. With electrical now in the picture, the framing needs to shift further: "Gardner Home Services" or "Gardner Plumbing, Electric and Air" (long but explicit) become the more honest representations of what the company does. This is a client conversation that should happen before the electrical vertical launches, not after. Flag to client-comms.

**GBP business name rule:** Do not change the GBP name field until a legal DBA is filed. GBP must match the legal/DBA name exactly. Stuffing "HVAC and Electric" into the GBP name field without a DBA is a suspension risk.

**Reviews are the trust bridge.** The practical mitigation sequence:

- HVAC reviews first. Get 5–8 HVAC-specific reviews on the profile before electrical launches — coordinate with ghl-engineer on the HVAC review request trigger post-job-completion. This establishes that Gardner does more than plumbing.
- Electrical photos at launch. Upload at minimum 8–10 electrical-specific photos (panel work, EV charger installs, code-compliant work documented) the day electrical services go live. Google's photo context signals matter for query-surface matching.
- Electrical reviews as quickly as possible post-launch. Do not let the electrical vertical run for more than 30 days without at least 2–3 reviews mentioning electrical work by name.

**Recommended launch sequence:**
1. HVAC soft launch (in flight)
2. HVAC review accumulation — 60–90 days
3. Electrical launch — only after HVAC has established the "we do more than plumbing" signal
4. DBA conversation with client — ideally resolved before or coincident with electrical launch

If the client insists on launching electrical simultaneously with HVAC, the risk is manageable but the review ramp must be deliberate and fast on both fronts.

## 5. GBP Post Timing — Electrical Launch Cadence

Inland Empire electrical demand patterns by season:

**Summer (June–September):** Pool and spa wiring, ceiling fans, whole-home surge protection (thunderstorm season is limited but AC circuit overloads happen), EV charger installation (peak EV purchase season). **Lead with pool/spa wiring and EV chargers in summer — these are Inland Empire-specific hooks that plumbing profiles almost never post about, which makes them differentiated.**

**Fall (October–November):** Home remodels and additions (contractors slow down, homeowners start indoor projects), electrical panel upgrades (ahead of holiday loads), generator interest begins as Santa Ana wind season picks up.

**Winter (December–February):** Generator installation peaks after first wind/rain events. Holiday lighting installation (a short window but high-intent). Electrical safety inspections tied to new year home-prep messaging.

**Spring (March–May):** New construction and remodeling electrical picks up. EV charger installs continue. Outlet and switch upgrades for spring home prep.

**Recommended electrical launch cadence (assuming 60–90 day delay after HVAC):**

If electrical launches in late Q3 or early Q4 2026, lead with generator installation and panel upgrades — fall/winter Inland Empire demand. Do not lead with lighting installation or smart home (lower urgency for the season).

If electrical launches in summer 2026 (simultaneous with HVAC, not recommended), lead with pool and spa wiring and EV chargers — the most seasonally aligned and most differentiated services.

**Auto-poster `industry_context` sequencing:** Do not update Gardner's `industry_context` to include electrical until electrical is actually live and the category is added to GBP. Posting about electrical services before the GBP profile reflects them creates a coherence gap. Update `industry_context` in two phases: HVAC added first, electrical added only when the electrical category is live.

## 6. Compound Vertical Expansion Risk — Two Verticals at Once

**Sequential is unambiguously safer. Here is the specific GBP risk of doing both at once:**

**Category dilution:** Every secondary category added to GBP slightly dilutes the relevance signal for the primary category and all others. Adding 4–5 new secondary categories (3 HVAC + 1–2 electrical) at the same time sends a large category signal change to Google's local algorithm. Google's local index takes 2–6 weeks to fully process category changes. If both verticals go live at the same time and rankings fluctuate during the indexing window, it is impossible to diagnose which category change caused the movement.

**Review relevance dilution:** All of Gardner's existing reviews are plumbing-specific. Expanding into two new verticals simultaneously means the profile looks diluted to any user reading through reviews. One new vertical can be explained; two at once looks like a company that has suddenly changed what it does.

**Photo library gap:** Launching two new verticals at once requires 10+ new photos for HVAC and 8–10 new photos for electrical, all uploaded within a short window. This is achievable but requires coordinated effort with creative-director. If only one vertical gets photos at launch, the other looks thin.

**Diagnosis problem:** If map pack rankings drop after simultaneous expansion, it is unclear whether HVAC categories, electrical categories, photo changes, or review velocity caused it. Sequential expansion creates a clean before/after for each variable.

**Recommendation: HVAC first, electrical 60–90 days later.** The specific timing:

- HVAC GBP category additions + page launch in the current in-flight phase
- 60-day window: accumulate HVAC reviews, add HVAC photos, let the category changes settle
- Electrical GBP category addition + page launch at the 60–90 day mark
- 30-day post-electrical window: add electrical photos, push electrical review requests

If the client pushes back on the delay, the minimum viable sequential gap is 30 days — enough for Google's local index to process the first category change before the second is introduced. Do not launch both on the same day.
